About Philippine Snakes

The Philippines is home to a diverse range of endemic snakes that play an essential role in maintaining ecosystem balance. From forest floors to rivers and mangroves, each species contributes uniquely to biodiversity.

Explore their habitats, behaviors, and learn how conservation helps protect these magnificent creatures.

Snake Categories

Non-Venomous

  • Luzon Brown Rat Snake — Slender brown snake; fast and agile.
  • Maren's Bronzeback — Bronze-green arboreal snake; cream underside.
  • Wolfsnake — Dark with pale crossbands; mimic of venomous kraits.
  • Banded Kukri Snake — Small egg eater snake; great various pattern

Mildly Venomous

  • Paradise Tree Snake — Colorful gliding snake; arboreal.
  • Asian Vine Snake — Thin green snake; excellent camouflage.
  • Philippine Pit Viper — Green viper with heat-sensing pits.
  • Dog-Toothed Cat Snake — Long arboreal snake; with sa viper like head

Highly Venomous

  • King Cobra — Longest venomous snake; iconic hood display.
  • Philippine Cobra — Dangerous spitting cobra; potent neurotoxin.
  • Batanes Pit Viper — Slendered white morphed viper; whitish to yellow coloration.
  • Samar Cobra — Medium-sized cobra; greenish to brown coloration.

Regions

Luzon

Home to non-venomous and mildly venomous snakes found in northern and central Philippines.

Visayas

Hosts unique harmless and mildly venomous snake species vital for biodiversity.

Mindanao

Contains highly venomous species along with non-venomous snakes, emphasizing conservation importance.
